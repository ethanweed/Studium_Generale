## Reliabilism vs. scepticism
### Method
Bird (pg. 145)
“On the reliabilist account, the method by which I acquire my beliefs must be reliable, in order that they should count as knowledge; but it is not necessary that I should know that it is reliable.”

Method

  • Reliabilism requires that an investigator use a reliable method to acquire knowledge.
  • She does not need to know that the method is reliable.
  • So what does it mean for a method to be “reliable”?
## Reliabilism 1.0 N knows that p if and only if - N believes that p; - it is true that p; and - N’s belief that p was acquired by a reliable method

Reliabilism 2.0

N knows that p if and only if - N believes that p; - it is true that p; and - N’s belief that p rests on a reliable method such that the explanation of the general reliability of the method also explains its success on this occasion.

Why does Bird say that beliefs acquired by a reliable method count as knowledge, but only if the method was not chosen at random?

Reliabilism in plain English (sort of)

You know something if the reason that you think something is true is because you used a reliable method to come to that conclusion, and the reason the method worked in this case is the same reason that it usually works.
